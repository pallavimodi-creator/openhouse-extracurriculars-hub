# pilot instrument 3 — experience book (v0)

A teacher-completed progress book, **one per child**, that travels across all three strand days and grows over the term. Modelled on the parent-toddler club `ExperienceBookDeck` (child-involved, warm, taken home), and grounded in NCF-FS's prescribed methods: **not a test** — qualitative teacher observation via checklists of observable markers, anecdotal records, and a child-work artefact portfolio taken home each term.

## the spine
- **Domains = NCF-FS's five** (+ a cross-cutting habits strip), so one book covers art, stem and language days.
- **Scale = openhouse's existing progression**: `starting out → getting there → going strong` (no numbers, no pass/fail).
- **Cadence:** the educator notes against markers continuously (most naturally in the closing circle), writes **one real "moment" per child per month**, and the artefact folder + book go home each term.
- **Child-involved:** the child adds their own artefact and a "my favourite thing" mark, mirroring the parent-toddler book's tone.

## one observable activity → many markers
NCF point we lean on: a single open-ended moment (e.g. closing-circle story recap) lets the educator observe several markers at once — sequencing, vocabulary, self-expression, listening. So the closing circle is the main capture window; the book is filled *from what already happened*, not from a separate test.

## the marker pages

Each domain is one spread: 4–6 observable markers, each marked `starting out / getting there / going strong`, with a line for a dated note. Markers are seeded from NCF-FS + Head Start ELOF.

### 1 · physical development (fine & gross motor)
- holds and controls tools (crayon, brush, scissors, tweezers) with growing precision
- threads / sticks / tears / folds with intent
- moves whole body with control — balance, stop/start, slow-motion
- hand-eye coordination in build/place tasks

### 2 · socio-emotional & ethical development
- names and shows feelings (uses the feelings strip)
- waits, takes turns, shares materials
- joins group play; offers and listens to others' ideas
- cares for materials and the shared space (tidies, returns things)
- shows kindness / helps a peer

### 3 · cognitive development (incl. numeracy & scientific reasoning)
- sorts, matches, and makes/extends patterns
- counts and compares quantities (more/less, numbers gym level)
- observes, predicts, and tests ("what will happen if…")
- solves a problem by trying, persisting, adjusting
- understands sequence / order of steps *(STEM + language overlap)*

### 4 · language & literacy development  *(richest — pilot strand)*
- listens to a story with attention and stays involved
- describes what's happening in a picture
- asks and answers questions about characters/events
- uses new vocabulary from the day's book
- **re-tells a story in correct sequence, showing cause-and-effect** (ELOF 60-mo marker)
- includes story bones in their own telling — character · setting · problem · attempt · resolution
- speaks to the group and shows audience awareness *(public-speaking seed)*

### 5 · aesthetic & cultural development (art, music, creativity)
- explores varied media/materials freely
- makes deliberate choices (colour, shape, placement)
- expresses an idea or feeling through their work
- responds to art/songs/stories (notices, has a favourite, says why)
- engages with rhyme, song and movement

### cross-cutting · positive learning habits
- focuses and sustains attention in a block
- keeps their morning **intention**
- persists when something is hard; tries another way
- self-regulates in transitions (settles after the calm-down ritual)

---

## the per-month "moment" (one line, real, specific)
Mirrors openhouse's report-card discipline: one **specific, observed** moment per child per month — *"On the 12th, Aarav re-told the whole goat story in order and added his own ending where the goats throw a party."* Not "doing well." If it could apply to any child, it isn't specific enough.

## the artefact pocket
Each term the child's work (or a photo of it) goes in a folder with the book and travels home — the NCF artefact-portfolio method. On a language day the artefact might be a play-writes page or a photo of a puppet retell.

---

## what to test in the pilot
- Can the educator mark markers **in-session** (mostly from the closing circle) without it feeling like assessment or eating the 20-min closing?
- Are the **language markers** observable on a normal language day, or too fine-grained?
- Does `starting out → getting there → going strong` give enough signal, or do we need a 4th "not yet seen"?
- Does the one-moment-per-month land as do-able (target: under ~3 min/child)?
- Parent reaction when the book + artefacts go home.

> Build note: v0 is the **marker content** above. The visual book (deck-style spreads, child-friendly, openhouse brand, lowercase headings) gets designed like `ExperienceBookDeck` once the markers survive the pilot.

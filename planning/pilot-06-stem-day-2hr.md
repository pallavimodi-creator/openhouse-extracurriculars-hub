# pilot instrument 6 — stem day · 2-hour session skeleton

**Stem · ages 3–5 · at-centre (120 min) · group of 6–10**

Same shared frame as the language and art days — only the middle changes. At-building this strand was ~90 min. On a stem day the open-ended heart of the day alternates: **imagine playground** (construction / building) on some days, **wonder world** (thematic, hands-on exploration) on others — never both in one session.

Design rules (NCF-FS): plan around attention span; **alternate quiet ↔ active and whole-group ↔ individual**; keep the open-ended build/explore block as the heart of the day.

---

## the 120 minutes

| # | block | min | grouping | energy | what happens |
|---|---|----|----|----|----|
| 1 | **opening circle time** | 15 | whole group | active→calm | greeting → song + movement → calendar/intention → one self-regulation game (same every day) |
| 2 | **logic lab** | 20 | whole → pairs | calm-focused | one logic/reasoning game (sorting, patterns, sequence) — rules explained once, deepens over the year |
| 3 | **break / snack** | 10 | — | free | snack + free movement |
| 4 | **imagine playground / wonder world — deepened** | 40 | small groups → individual | active-focused | the heart of the day — build → test → explain (see deepening guide). IP and WW alternate by day. |
| 5 | **numbers gym** | 20 | individual | calm-focused | self-paced personal number gamebook (each child at their own level) |
| 6 | **closing & experience book** | 15 | whole → individual | calm | recap → feelings check → calm-down; teacher marks the experience book + child adds an artefact/photo of their build |

**= 120 min.** (+30 over at-building: circle 15 · break 10 · build/explore +5.) Minutes tune in the pilot.

Quiet/active check: active(1) → focused(2) → free(3) → active-focused(4) → quiet(5) → calm(6). Good spread; the busy build block (4) sits between the break and the quieter numbers gym.

---

## the deepened block — how to add time to the build/explore *meaningfully*

The extra time is **not** "build a bigger tower" — it's the **scientific-reasoning + engineering loop**, in three moves (mirrors language and art):

1. **build / explore (≈22 min)** — children build with the construction kit (imagine playground) or investigate the day's theme/materials (wonder world). Open-ended, child-led; teacher sets the challenge then steps back. Four questions only, never the answer.
2. **test (≈10 min)** — "what happens if…?" — push the tower, roll the ball down the ramp, change one thing and watch. This is observe → predict → test, the heart of early science. Children notice, compare, and try again.
3. **explain (≈8 min)** — each child (or group) shows their build and says one thing they found — "it fell because the bottom was too small", "the long ramp was faster." Putting a finding into words is where the thinking gets locked in — and it's the concept-question moment.

> Same three-move shape as language (do → look → talk) and art (make → look → talk). On stem days it reads as **build · test · explain**.

---

## what to watch during the stem pilot (feeds the session log)
- Does the 40-min build block sustain, or does it need a mid-point "test" prompt to re-focus?
- Do the **test** and **explain** moves actually happen, or does building eat the whole block? (they're the part that makes it stem, not just play)
- Is **logic-lab-before-build** the right order, or should the calmer logic game come after the busy build?
- Does numbers gym at the end (block 5) work when children are tiring, or should it move earlier?
- IP vs WW days — do they need different timings?

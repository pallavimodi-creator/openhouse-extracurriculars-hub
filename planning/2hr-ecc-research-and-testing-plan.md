# 2-hour at-centre ECC programme — research base, draft structure & testing plan

Ages 3–5 · one integrated programme → three rotating single-subject strands (art & design · stem · language), **one strand per day** · positioned as the foundation feeding the 5+ programmes (art & design · robotics · public speaking). Music & fitness = future 4th strand.

---

## 1. Evidence base (verified deep-research findings)

Sources: India NCF Foundational Stage / NEP 2020, Head Start ELOF, EYFS (UK), peer-reviewed ECE research. Claims below were adversarially verified (3-vote).

**Frameworks & holistic domains**
- NCF-FS organises early learning into 5 domains that map to our holistic domains: Physical · Socio-emotional & ethical · Cognitive (incl. foundational numeracy) · Language & literacy · Aesthetic & cultural — plus a cross-cutting goal: **Positive Learning Habits**. [ncf.ncert.gov.in; cdnbbsr.s3waas.gov.in]
- ELOF (5 domains): Approaches to Learning · Social-Emotional · Language & Literacy · Cognition · Perceptual/Motor/Physical. For ages 3–5 Cognition splits into **Mathematics Development** + **Scientific Reasoning** — a clean anchor for the STEM strand. [headstart.gov]
- EYFS: 3 prime areas (communication & language; physical; PSED) + 4 specific (literacy, maths, understanding the world, **expressive arts & design**). Maps directly onto our three strands. [gov.uk]
- NCF-FS explicitly makes **play the core pedagogy** that develops all domains at once, tying art-making, building/STEM, and storytelling/language to learning. [ncf.ncert.gov.in]

**The learning ladder (3–5 → 5+)**
- Art (EYFS Expressive Arts & Design): imagination, creativity, self-expression, communication through hands-on media → **art & design**. [gov.uk]
- STEM: children as young as 4 grasp basic programming/robotics; early robotics builds fine motor, hand-eye, collaboration, computational thinking & the engineering design process; a concrete gateway to applied maths & inquiry → **robotics**. [ecrp.illinois.edu]; CT can be built screen-free via ordered-steps/sequence activities [naeyc.org].
- Language/storytelling: oral storytelling is one of the most effective ways to build oral language; teaches story elements (character, sequence, plot, resolution), word choice and **audience awareness**; strong preschool narrative predicts later reading/writing → **public speaking & communication**. Read-aloud + story-grammar instruction measurably improved preschoolers' storytelling (RCT, n=47). [easternct.edu; link.springer.com]

**Session design (pacing)**
- NCF-FS illustrative daily routine uses timed blocks (45-min circle time, 15-min snack break, rhyme/song/movement, concept time, arts/craft, story time, wind-up) and prescribes **planning around the child's short attention span**: alternate quiet↔active, group↔individual, indoor↔outdoor; balance child-initiated with teacher-guided. [ncf.ncert.gov.in]

**Observation / progress tracking (for the experience book)**
- NCF-FS prescribes teacher-completed, **non-test** methods: anecdotal records, **checklists of observable markers**, event/time sampling, and **artefacts kept in a portfolio folder taken home each term**. [ncf.ncert.gov.in; cdnbbsr.s3waas.gov.in]
- A single open-ended activity (e.g. circle-time storytelling) lets a teacher observe **multiple cross-domain markers at once** — sequencing, vocabulary, self-expression, connection to environment. [cdnbbsr.s3waas.gov.in]
- ELOF gives concrete language markers usable verbatim (e.g. by 60 months, re-tells a story in correct sequence showing cause-and-effect). [headstart.gov]

> Takeaway: our instinct (shared circle time + break + a deepened core block + a closing/reflection that doubles as observation) is directly supported by NCF-FS — the 2-hour structure sits on India's official Foundational Stage framework, not just generic best practice.

## 2. Draft 2-hour daily frame (the thing we're testing)

A single **shared frame** wraps whichever strand is on that day (one strand/day). The +30–40 min over the 1.5-hr at-building version comes from the shaded rows.

| Block | Time | What it is | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Opening circle time** | 15 min | greeting · song/movement · calendar/intention · 1 self-regulation game | **NEW (shared)** |
| Core block 1 | ~20–25 min | strand warm-up / skill game | existing |
| **Break / snack** | 10 min | snack + free movement | **NEW (shared)** |
| **Deepened core block** | ~30–40 min | read-aloud (lang) · art-making (art) · imagine-playground/wonder-world (stem) | **+10 deepened** |
| Core block 3 | ~20 min | strand skill block (numbers gym / play-writes / art care, etc.) | existing |
| **Closing & experience book** | 15 min | recap · feelings check · calm-down + teacher marks the experience book / child adds an artefact | **closing NEW + existing book** |

Per-strand mapping (minutes to be tuned in the pilot — that's what testing is for):
- **Art day:** circle → art gym+games → break → **artiverse making (deepened)** → art care → closing+book
- **Language day:** circle → roll&rhyme + wordsmiths → break → **book'o'clock read-aloud (deepened)** → play-writes + playground game → closing+book *(needs the most added time — at-building was only ~80 min)*
- **STEM day:** circle → logic lab → break → **imagine playground / wonder world (deepened)** → numbers gym → closing+book

Design rules from the research: alternate quiet↔active across blocks; keep any single teacher-led stretch within attention span; the deepened block is the one open-ended, child-led part of the day.

## 3. Testing / pilot plan

**Goal:** validate the 2-hour structure (pacing, the new circle/break/closing, the deepened block, and the experience book) with real children + educators before building all content and graduating the strands from "trial" to "live" in the hub.

### 3.1 What we're testing (hypotheses)
1. **Pacing fits** — 120 min holds 3–5s without overload; the break lands at the right point; deepened blocks sustain engagement rather than sag.
2. **The shared frame works** — circle time and closing are run-able by an educator and add value (focus at start, regulation + reflection at end).
3. **The experience book is usable** — an educator can mark observable markers within the 15-min closing without it feeling like a test.
4. **Parents get the positioning** — the "why art/stem/language + what it builds toward" story lands.

### 3.2 Scope
- **Duration:** 4 weeks (≈ enough to cycle each strand several times and iterate weekly).
- **Cadence:** one strand per day, rotating across the week.
- **Group:** one centre group, 6–10 children (the centre class size), 1–2 educators.
- **Per strand:** at least 3 sessions each over the pilot so we see a strand on a good day and a hard day.

### 3.3 What we measure (success criteria)
| Dimension | Signal | How captured |
|---|---|---|
| Engagement & flow | % of children engaged per block; where attention drops | educator session log (per-block 1–5 + note) |
| Pacing | did each block run to time? what dragged / felt rushed? | educator session log |
| Transitions | smooth vs chaotic between blocks (key 3–5 risk) | educator log + observer note |
| Educator usability | could they run circle/closing/book in the time? | educator debrief (weekly) |
| Developmental signal | were the experience-book markers actually observable & fillable? | the experience book itself |
| Parent perception | do parents understand & value the positioning? | short parent pulse (start + end) |
| Artefacts | child work captured for the portfolio | NCF-style artefact folder |

### 3.4 Instruments to build for the pilot (minimum)
- **Educator session log** (one page/day): per-block timing + engagement + free note + transitions.
- **Experience book v0** — observable markers organised by the 5 holistic domains (seeded from NCF/ELOF markers), modelled on the parent-toddler `ExperienceBookDeck`.
- **Circle-time & closing-time activity set v0** — ~5 opening + ~5 closing routines to rotate.
- **Deepened-block guidance** — how to extend read-aloud / art-making / build by ~10 min meaningfully (open-ended extension, not more worksheet).
- **Parent pulse** — 3–4 questions on clarity of value + child enjoyment.

### 3.5 Loop & decision
- **Weekly review:** read the logs → adjust block timings + swap weak activities → re-run next week.
- **Go/no-go to "live":** a strand graduates from the hub's *trial* track to *live* when pacing is stable, the experience book is fillable in-session, and educator + parent feedback clear an agreed bar.

### 3.6 Build order (so we can start the pilot fast)
1. Educator session log + experience book v0 + circle/closing set v0 (pilot instruments).
2. Run week 1 (start with the strand we're most ready on).
3. Iterate weekly.
4. In parallel, draft the **parent positioning** doc (Part A) for the pulse + eventual overview.
5. After the pilot stabilises: build the validated structure, infographic, positioning and experience book into the hub and flip strands to *live*.

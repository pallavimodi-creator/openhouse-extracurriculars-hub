## 1 · the goal (and the one big why)

**parent-facing one line:**
> *"your child thinks like a little scientist and engineer — wonders, builds, tries, and works things out — and grows a real sense of numbers."*

**the one big why — school readiness.** Stem here is **not** worksheets or early academics. It builds the **thinking** school stands on:
- **a curious, problem-solving mind** — a child who notices, asks "what if," tries, and keeps going. (These are NCF's *positive learning habits* and the heart of all later learning.)
- **number sense** — not rote counting, but *understanding* what numbers mean: quantity, more/less, making numbers in different ways, and early operations — the true foundation of school maths.
- **scientific reasoning** — observe → predict → test → explain — the loop behind all science.

So "how do children become ready for school?" — they arrive **able to think, to persist, and to reason about quantity**, which is what makes maths and science *make sense* rather than be memorised.

## 2 · the skills (the spine) — your existing four, deepened

Each skill keeps its at-building ability ladder → ★ north star.

| skill | what it really is | ability ladder → ★ |
|---|---|---|
| **curiosity** | notices, wonders, predicts, investigates | notices → wonders → predicts → **investigates ★** |
| **problem solving** | recognises a problem, tries, persists, solves | recognises → tries → persists → **solves ★** |
| **logic** | sorts, finds patterns, reasons from a rule | sorts → patterns → predicts-from-a-rule → **reasons ★** |
| **number sense** | connects number to quantity, relates numbers, applies | connects → relates → **applies ★** |

> scientific reasoning (observe · predict · test · explain) is **not a fifth skill — it's how curiosity + problem solving run** in the build/explore block (imagine playground / wonder world). Named so it actually gets taught.

## 3 · number sense — the milestone spiral (your sheet is the source of truth)

This is the strand the milestones matter most for. Your **11-month number-sense spiral** *is* the LO ladder — activities and the experience book are planned to move each child up it. Here it is mapped onto the three levels; the **month-by-month detail lives in your spiral sheet** (Number Recognition & Quantity · Number Relationships · Place Value).

| | **L1 · connects** | **L2 · relates** | **L3 · applies ★** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **recognition & quantity** | counts & recognises numbers with 1-1 correspondence (to ~10–20) 👁 *counts out 5–10 objects one by one* | counts forward to 20–30; continues from any number; records quantities 👁 *continues counting from 14 without restarting* | counts to 50; counts-on across decades (29→30); counts independently to check "enough" 👁 *counts items to check there are enough for the group* |
| **number relationships** | says which set has more / less / equal by looking 👁 *picks the bigger set* | knows the number before & after; **makes a number in 2+ ways** (8 = 5+3 = 6+2) 👁 *builds 8 two ways* | solves **join & take-away within 20** by counting-on (not rebuilding) 👁 *solves 7 + 3 by counting on from 7* |
| **place value** | — | identifies a complete group of **ten** (ten-frame) 👁 *points to the ten* | **bundles** tens & loose ones (11–50); compares two-digit numbers by bundles 👁 *makes a bundle of 10 and leaves the rest* |

**★ number-sense north star (3–5):** *understands what numbers mean — counts with meaning, makes numbers in different ways, and solves simple join/take-away by counting-on.*

> your "5-year-old does addition, 3-year-old learns numbers" — that's exactly this table: in **one** numbers-gym game or logic-lab activity, the L1 child counts to 5 while the L3 child solves 7 + 3, **same activity, different rung.**

### LO ladders — the other three skills (by level, each with a 👁 check)

| skill | L1 (going strong) | L2 (going strong) | L3 ★ (going strong) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **curiosity** | notices something new & reacts 👁 *points it out* | asks "what if…" / wonders aloud 👁 *a question* | predicts before testing & investigates to find out 👁 *says what they think will happen, then checks* |
| **problem solving** | recognises something isn't working 👁 *stops & looks* | tries a different way 👁 *a second attempt* | persists & solves; says what helped 👁 *keeps going to a solution & explains* |
| **logic** | sorts & matches by one feature 👁 *a correct sort* | makes & extends a pattern 👁 *continues a pattern* | predicts from a rule / reasons it out 👁 *says the next one & why* |

## 4 · levels — same activity, every child at their own rung

Three levels within 3–5, **per-skill attainment, age-agnostic** (a child can be L3 in logic, L1 in number sense). The **same activity runs at every level at once** by changing the rung. Promotion = securing the ★ rung independently, more than once — by attainment, **not birthday**.

The build/explore block (imagine playground / wonder world) and numbers gym are inherently leveled: open-ended building lets each child work at their edge; the personal numbers-gym gamebook is **self-paced by design** (each child at their own page).

## 5 · how it feeds the rest

- **experience book:** the number-sense **spiral milestones** and the three skills' rungs are exactly what the book tracks per child — the dot-and-date journey toward each ★, with the "needs more help" circle. The book is *planned from these milestones* (your words).
- **activities (already built):** logic lab games, imagine playground builds, wonder world investigations, numbers gym gamebook — each runs at all three rungs. The **new segments** to script are only the shared **opening circle / closing reflection** and the **deepened build block moves** (build → test → explain, keyed to curiosity/problem-solving/scientific-reasoning rungs).

### format-set principle (for the new segments)
Same as language: each new recurring slot gets a **small fixed set of named, scripted formats**, rotated on a known schedule — repetition for the child, no guesswork for the teacher, each format nudging a different sub-skill. (e.g. a fixed set of 4–5 **"what-if" prompts** to open the build block; a fixed set of **calm-down** rituals to close.)

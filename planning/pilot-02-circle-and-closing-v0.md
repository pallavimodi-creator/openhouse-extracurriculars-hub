# pilot instrument 2 — circle-time & closing-time activity set (v0)

The two **shared rituals** that bookend every strand day (art, stem, language). Same structure daily — only the song/topic flexes to the day's strand — so children get a predictable, regulating frame. Grounded in NCF-FS (circle time as the daily opener; "alternate quiet ↔ active"; plan around attention span) and the holistic-development domains.

Run **5–6 short moves** in each bookend, not one long activity. Keep each move 2–4 min; total ≈ 15 min open, ≈ 10–15 min close (the rest of closing is the experience book).

Domains tracked: **PSED** (personal-social-emotional) · **C&L** (communication & language) · **PD** (physical) · **CL** (positive learning habits / self-regulation) · **AC** (aesthetic/cultural).

---

## opening circle time (~15 min)

| move | ~min | how to run | builds |
|---|---|---|---|
| **good morning, name by name** | 3 | sing/say a fixed greeting going around the circle; each child is greeted by name and answers one tiny prompt ("show me your hello — wave, clap, or stomp"). | PSED · C&L · belonging |
| **song & shake-out** | 3 | one action song with whole-body movement (on language day, use today's *roll & rhyme* song). gets wiggles out before sitting work. | PD · C&L · AC |
| **today's window** | 3 | calendar + weather + "what's today" — point to day/weather cards; one child is "weather reporter". then name the day's strand in kid words ("today is a *making* day / *story* day / *building* day"). | C&L · cognitive · agency |
| **our intention** | 2 | one shared, tiny intention for the day ("today we'll be good *listeners*"), said together with a hand gesture. revisited at closing. | CL (learning habits) · PSED |
| **the focus game** | 3–4 | one self-regulation / attention game — rotate from the bank below. | CL (self-regulation) · PD |

**focus-game bank (rotate):**
- **statue / freeze** — dance to music, freeze when it stops (inhibition control).
- **my turn, your turn (echo)** — teacher claps/says a pattern, children echo (attention, working memory).
- **slow-motion** — do an action in slow motion on cue (impulse control).
- **listening detective** — close eyes, name the sound the teacher makes (auditory attention).
- **simon says (openhouse version)** — only act on "openhouse says" (inhibition, listening).

## closing & reflection time (~10–15 min, before/around the experience book)

| move | ~min | how to run | builds |
|---|---|---|---|
| **the recap circle** | 3 | "what did we do today?" — children name the parts in order (this also rehearses **sequencing**). on a story day, retell the story bones. | C&L · cognitive (sequence) |
| **did we keep our intention?** | 2 | revisit the morning intention with a thumbs check ("were we good listeners?"). | CL · PSED · self-assessment |
| **feelings check** | 3 | each child points to a feeling face (visual feelings strip) for how today felt, optionally one word why. | PSED (emotional literacy) |
| **one good thing (gratitude/share)** | 2–3 | each child shares one thing they liked or are proud of — short, no pressure, pass allowed. mini show-and-tell that builds **audience awareness**. | PSED · C&L (public speaking seed) |
| **calm-down & goodbye** | 2–3 | one breathing/calming ritual (rotate below) + a fixed goodbye song/handshake so the transition home is regulated, not chaotic. | CL · PD · transition |

**calm-down bank (rotate):**
- **balloon breath** — breathe in arms-up (inflate), out arms-down (deflate), x3.
- **smell the flower, blow the candle** — slow nose-in, mouth-out.
- **five-finger trace** — trace up/down each finger with breath.
- **starfish stretch** — big stretch then melt down small.

---

## why this matters for the pilot
- The bookends are the **only fully new content** the 2-hour frame needs — so they're the first thing to test for run-ability and timing.
- The **recap circle, feelings check, and "one good thing"** double as live observation moments — the educator can mark experience-book markers (sequencing, emotional expression, speaking to a group) straight from them.
- Watch: can a single educator run 5 moves in the time? which focus/calm games actually land with this group? does the fixed goodbye reduce the end-of-day chaos?

# pilot instrument 1 — language day · 2-hour session skeleton

**Language through storytelling · ages 3–5 · at-centre (120 min) · group of 6–10**

This is the concrete language-day plan the pilot runs. At-building this strand was ~80 min; the centre version rebuilds it to 120 using the shared daily frame. The shared opening/closing/break are identical on every strand day — only the middle (the strand content) changes.

Design rules carried from the research (NCF-FS): plan each block around the child's attention span; **alternate quiet ↔ active and whole-group ↔ individual**; keep one open-ended, child-led block as the heart of the day (here, the read-aloud + response).

---

## the 120 minutes

| # | block | min | grouping | energy | what happens |
|---|---|----|----|----|----|
| 1 | **opening circle time** | 15 | whole group | active→calm | greeting → a *roll & rhyme* song + movement → calendar/intention → one self-regulation game. (Roll & Rhyme folds into circle here — no separate song block.) |
| 2 | **wordsmiths** | 15 | whole → pairs | calm | today's vocabulary via see → act → say (emotion tiles / story calendar), tied to today's book |
| 3 | **break / snack** | 10 | — | free | snack + free movement; reset before the long block |
| 4 | **book'o'clock — deepened read-aloud** | 35 | whole → small | calm→active | the heart of the day — dialogic read-aloud + story-grammar + illustration talk + a group response (see deepening guide below) |
| 5 | **play-writes + playground game** | 25 | individual → whole | active | individual A4 pre-writing book, then one language playground game |
| 6 | **closing & experience book** | 20 | whole → individual | calm | story recap → feelings check → calm-down ritual; educator marks the experience book + child adds an artefact to the portfolio |

**= 120 min.** (Language's +40 over at-building comes from: circle 15 · break 10 · read-aloud deepened +10 · fuller closing.)

Quiet/active rhythm check: active(1) → calm(2) → free(3) → calm-with-active-peak(4) → active(5) → calm(6). Good — no two heavy quiet blocks back to back.

---

## the deepened block — how to add 10 minutes to the read-aloud *meaningfully*

The at-building read-aloud is ~25 min. The extra 10 is **not** "read more pages" — it's a research-backed deepening. From the verified RCT (read-aloud + story-grammar + illustration/visual-design instruction measurably improved preschoolers' oral storytelling), the deepened block runs in three moves:

1. **Dialogic read-aloud (≈15 min)** — read *with*, not *at*. Pause to ask open prompts ("what do you think happens next? why is she feeling that way?"), let children answer, expand on what they say, repeat the richer version back. The book stays the spine (6-day arc as today).
2. **Illustration & story-grammar talk (≈10 min)** — go back to 2–3 spreads and talk about the *pictures* (what do you see? how did the artist show it was night/scary/happy?) and name the **story bones**: who (character) · where (setting) · what went wrong (problem) · what they did (attempt) · how it ended (resolution). Use a simple 5-icon story-bones strip children can point to.
3. **Group response (≈10 min)** — children re-tell or change the story together (story re-enactment, change-the-ending, puppet retell, or "tell it from the wolf's side"). This is where oral storytelling — the skill that ladders to public speaking — actually gets practised, with audience awareness.

> The same three-move pattern is what the art day (make → look closely → talk about choices) and stem day (build → test → explain) will use for *their* deepened blocks. We validate it on language first.

## what to watch during the language pilot (feeds the session log)
- Does the 35-min read-aloud block **sag**, or does the dialogic + response structure hold it?
- Is **wordsmiths-before-break** the right order, or should vocabulary come *after* the story it belongs to?
- Can the educator get through circle's four parts in 15 min?
- Does the closing's story-recap give a natural, low-effort moment to mark the experience book?

# activity pack — opening circle time
### shared · runs the same way on every strand day · ~15 min · whole group on the mat

This is the start of every day. Same five moves, same order, every day — the *sameness is the point* (it settles and focuses 3–5s). Only the **song** and the **strand word** change by day. Scripted so a brand-new educator can run it word-for-word.

**the five moves (in order):** greeting → song → today's window → intention → focus game.
**materials (kept in the circle box):** name cards · weather/day cards · feelings strip (also used at closing) · the focus-game props (music/speaker, a few picture cards).

---

## move 1 · greeting — "good morning, name by name" *(~3 min)*
**skill:** speaking · belonging (learning habits)
**say (sung or spoken to a steady clap):**
> *"Good morning [name], good morning [name], we're so glad you're here today!"* — go around the circle, **greet each child by name.**
Each child answers with **one tiny choice:** *"show me your hello — a wave, a clap, or a stomp."*
**why:** every child is named and seen in the first 2 minutes; the choice gives a quick, safe turn to speak.

---

## move 2 · song & shake-out *(~3 min)*
**skill:** listening · vocabulary (rhyme) · physical (movement)
Play **today's roll & rhyme song** (your existing 5-song playlist — *don't pick a new one*). Do the actions together standing up — this gets the wiggles out before sitting work.

**the fixed weekly song rota (your existing roll & rhyme set):**
| day | song |
|---|---|
| Mon | Here We Go Round the Mulberry Bush |
| Tue | If You're Happy and You Know It |
| Wed | Walking Through the Jungle |
| Thu | Knick Knack Paddy Whack (This Old Man) |
| Fri | There's a Hole in the Bottom of the Sea |
> same 5 all year → children master and *anticipate* them (security), you never have to choose. (On art/stem days, use the same rota — the song is the shared opener, not the subject.)

---

## move 3 · today's window *(~3 min)*
**skill:** speaking · cognitive (sequence/time) · agency
**say:** *"Let's open our window on today."* One child is **weather reporter** (point to the weather card). Together: *"Today is ______ (day). The weather is ______."*
Then name the strand in **kid words:**
> *"And today is a **____ day** — a story day / a making day / a building day!"*
**why:** orients the child in time and tells them what kind of day to expect.

---

## move 4 · our intention *(~2 min)*
**skill:** learning habits · social-emotional
Pick **one tiny intention** for the day and say it together with a hand gesture (you revisit it at closing).
**the fixed intention set (rotate / pick to fit the day):**
1. *"today we'll be good **listeners**"* (hand cupped to ear)
2. *"today we'll be **kind** to our friends"* (hand on heart)
3. *"today we'll **try our best**, even when it's tricky"* (fist of determination)
4. *"today we'll **take turns**"* (hands passing)
5. *"today we'll **look closely**"* (binoculars hands)
> keep it tiny and doable — one habit, named, that you can honestly check at closing.

---

## move 5 · the focus game *(~3–4 min)*
**skill:** self-regulation / attention (learning habits) · physical
One short game that wakes up attention and impulse-control. **A fixed set of 5 — one per weekday** so it's never a guess and children get repetition:

| day | game | how to run (say it, then play) | builds |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | **statue / freeze** | "dance while the music plays — **freeze** like a statue when it stops!" play 3–4 rounds. | impulse control (stopping) |
| Tue | **my turn, your turn (echo)** | "watch and listen, then copy me." clap/say a short pattern; children echo it back. make it a little longer each round. | attention · working memory |
| Wed | **slow motion** | "let's all wave… in **sloooow motion**." do 2–3 actions in slow motion on your cue. | impulse control · body control |
| Thu | **listening detective** | "close your eyes — what sound do you hear?" make a sound (shaker, clap, knock); children guess. | auditory attention |
| Fri | **openhouse says** | like Simon Says — only do the action if you say **"openhouse says."** | listening · inhibition |
> same 5 all year. Each targets a slightly different attention sub-skill, and the child anticipates the day's game (which itself builds the security and self-regulation 3–5s need).

---

## the exit ritual (into the first block)
End circle with the **same line every day**, so the transition is clean:
> *"circle is done — let's tiptoe to our ______ (story mat / making table / building corner)."*
(Transitions are the #1 chaos point for 3–5s — a fixed exit line is your friend.)

> **observe nothing to mark here** — circle is for settling, not assessment. You'll mark skills at closing. (Though if a child nails a focus game or speaks up in greeting, you *may* note speaking/listening for your focus children.)

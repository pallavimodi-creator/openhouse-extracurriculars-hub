# activity pack — closing & reflection time
### shared · runs the same way on every strand day · ~15 min · whole group on the mat

The end of every day. Same five moves, same order. This block does double duty: it **settles** the child for pick-up **and** it's your main **observe window** for the experience book — the recap and one-good-thing let you watch *speaking · sequencing · storytelling* for your focus children without any "testing."

**the five moves (in order):** recap → intention check → feelings check → one good thing → calm-down & goodbye.
**materials:** the feelings strip · the experience book (you mark a few children during/after).

---

## move 1 · the recap circle *(~3 min)*
**skill:** speaking · cognitive (sequence) · — *(this is an observe window)*
**say:** *"What did we do today? Let's remember it in order."* Children name the parts of the day in sequence. On a **story day**, instead retell the story bones together: *"who was in it? where? what went wrong? what did they do? how did it end?"*
**why:** rehearses **sequencing** and **recall** — and on story days, the story-bones (the storytelling north star). 👁 *watch your 2–4 focus children: can they put it in order? → mark speaking / storytelling.*

---

## move 2 · did we keep our intention? *(~2 min)*
**skill:** learning habits · social-emotional (self-assessment)
**say:** *"This morning we said we'd be ______. Did we?"* Thumbs up / thumbs sideways together. Name one moment it happened (*"I saw Riya share her crayons — that was kind!"*).
**why:** closes the loop opened at circle; builds self-awareness and names good behaviour specifically.

---

## move 3 · feelings check *(~3 min)*
**skill:** social-emotional (emotional literacy)
**say:** *"How did today feel for you?"* Each child points to a face on the **feelings strip** (happy · excited · calm · tired · sad · cross). Optional: one word why. **Pass is allowed.**
**why:** names emotions — core school-readiness self-regulation. 👁 *a child who can name a feeling = social-emotional progress.*

---

## move 4 · one good thing *(~2–3 min)*
**skill:** speaking (to the group — public-speaking seed) · social-emotional (gratitude)
**say:** *"Tell us **one thing** you liked or are proud of today."* Quick go-around, short, **pass allowed.** A mini show-and-tell — they speak to the group about *their* day.
**why:** builds **audience awareness** (the seed of public speaking) and ends the day on pride. 👁 *watch focus children: do they speak up to the group? → mark speaking.*

---

## move 5 · calm-down & goodbye *(~3 min)*
**skill:** self-regulation (learning habits) · physical
One breathing/calming ritual, then the **same goodbye every day** so pick-up is calm, not chaotic.

**the calm-down set (fixed 4 — one per part of the week, rotate):**
| ritual | how to run (say + do) |
|---|---|
| **balloon breath** | "breathe in — fill your balloon (arms up big), breathe out — let it go (arms down)." ×3 |
| **smell the flower, blow the candle** | "smell the flower (slow in through the nose), blow the candle (slow out through the mouth)." ×3 |
| **five-finger trace** | "trace up and down each finger — breathe in going up, out coming down." |
| **starfish to pebble** | "big starfish stretch… now melt down small like a little pebble." |

**the goodbye (same every day):**
> *"Today was a good day. Hands on heart — see you tomorrow, openhouse friends!"* (a fixed wave or handshake)

---

## after the children leave — fill the book (~2 min)
While it's fresh: write the **daily page** (strand · what we did · one moment) and **date the dots** for the 2–4 focus children you watched in the recap / one-good-thing.

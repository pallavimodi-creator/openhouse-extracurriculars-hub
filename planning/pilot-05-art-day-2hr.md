# pilot instrument 5 — art day · 2-hour session skeleton

**Art & design · ages 3–5 · at-centre (120 min) · group of 6–10**

Same shared frame as the language day — only the middle changes. At-building this strand was ~90 min; the centre version adds the shared circle, break, a deepened making block, and a fuller closing.

Design rules (NCF-FS): plan around attention span; **alternate quiet ↔ active and whole-group ↔ individual**; keep one open-ended, child-led block as the heart of the day (here, artiverse making).

---

## the 120 minutes

| # | block | min | grouping | energy | what happens |
|---|---|----|----|----|----|
| 1 | **opening circle time** | 15 | whole group | active→calm | greeting → song + movement → calendar/intention → one self-regulation game (same as every day) |
| 2 | **art gym + art games** | 25 | whole → pairs | calm→active | fine-motor warm-up (laminated art-gym book / cue cards) + one art game |
| 3 | **break / snack** | 10 | — | free | snack + free movement |
| 4 | **artiverse — deepened making** | 40 | individual | calm-focused | the heart of the day — make → look closely → talk about choices (see deepening guide) |
| 5 | **art care** | 10 | whole | calm | tidy + return materials + the "i care" ritual (responsibility, slowing down) |
| 6 | **closing & experience book** | 20 | whole → individual | calm | recap → feelings check → calm-down; educator marks the experience book + child adds their artwork to the portfolio |

**= 120 min.** (+30 over at-building: circle 15 · break 10 · making +5 · closing +5 / art-care +5.) Minutes tune in the pilot.

Quiet/active check: active(1) → mixed(2) → free(3) → focused-quiet(4) → calm(5) → calm(6). The one risk is three calmer blocks at the end (4–6) — watch for fatigue; the closing's feelings-check + calm-down should re-energise, not flatten.

---

## the deepened block — how to add time to art-making *meaningfully*

The extra time is **not** "make a second thing" — it's depth, in three moves (mirrors the language day's pattern):

1. **make (≈25 min)** — the artwork, with the day's medium and technique. Real materials, real choices, no fixed "correct" outcome. Educator sets up then steps back.
2. **look closely (≈8 min)** — a short "gallery look": children walk past or hold up work and *notice* — "what did you use? how did you make that bit? what's different about everyone's?" Looking is a skill; this is where observation grows.
3. **talk about choices (≈7 min)** — each child says one thing about *their* choice — "i made the sky purple because…", "i used my fingers, not the brush." This builds the language of art and self-expression — and rehearses speaking about your own work (a quiet public-speaking seed).

> Same three-move shape as language (make/do → look → talk) and stem (build → test → explain). The deepened block is always: *do it · notice it · say something about it.*

---

## what to watch during the art pilot (feeds the session log)
- Does the 40-min making block hold, or do younger children finish and drift? (the "look + talk" moves should catch them)
- Is **art care** a calm reset or a chaotic scramble before closing? does it need its own clearer ritual?
- Can the educator run the "look closely / talk about choices" moves, or does making eat the whole 40?
- Three calmer blocks at the end — is the group fading? does the break need to move later?

# art & design — programme & formats note
### the teacher source-of-truth · ages 3–5 · at-centre

> **Output ①** — *goal · skills · learning outcomes · milestones · levels · format map.* The **experience book**, the **activities** and **teacher training** derive from it. Skill spine + activities already exist in the at-building programme (fine motor · colour · creative expression; art gym · art games · reference-artwork making · art care) — this note adds the **goal/why**, the **levels & milestones**, and the **format-set** layer.

---

## 1 · the goal (and the one big why)

**parent-facing one line:**
> *"your child's hands, eyes and imagination grow together — they look closely, make freely, and learn to say what they made and why."*

**the one big why — school readiness.** Art here is not "craft" or copying a sample. It builds three school foundations at once:
- **fine-motor control** — the hand that paints, threads and draws is the **same hand that will write.** Strong fine motor is *writing readiness* (this is why the language strand's pre-writing and the art strand reinforce each other).
- **looking & observation** — children learn to *notice* (colour, shape, detail) — the attention that underpins all careful learning.
- **creative confidence & self-expression** — making real choices, expressing an idea or feeling, and the resilience of "there's no wrong answer." This is the divergent thinking and emotional literacy school rarely teaches but always needs.

So "how do children become ready for school?" — **ready hands, noticing eyes, and the confidence to make and explain a choice.**

> note on "reference artworks": the making block shows children a **reference** artwork for the age — *not* a sample to copy. Children make **their own**; educators pick the artwork. The skill is expression and choice, never reproduction.

---

## 2 · the skills (the spine) — your existing three, each a 4-rung ladder → ★

| skill | what it really is | ability ladder → ★ |
|---|---|---|
| **fine motor** | hand control with tools — the writing-ready hand | tool precision → tracing → drawing figures & patterns → **fine-motor integration ★** |
| **colour** | exploring, naming, mixing, using colour with intent | explores freely → names & recognises → mixes & notices → **colour integration ★** |
| **creative expression** | ideas, feelings and deliberate artistic choices | explores concepts → emotional expression → integrating choices → **visual-arts integration ★** |

---

## 3 · LO ladders by level — each rung with a 👁 check

The four named rungs *are* the LO ladder; **level = the rung a child is working at** (L1 ≈ rungs 1–2 · L2 ≈ rung 3 · L3 ≈ rung 4 ★), per-skill and age-agnostic.

| skill | starting out | getting there | going strong | ★ north star |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **fine motor** | holds & controls a tool, makes deliberate marks 👁 *a chosen mark, good grip* | traces a line/shape staying on the path 👁 *traces a shape* | draws recognisable shapes & figures, repeats patterns 👁 *draws a person/house from memory* | combines control in a detailed piece 👁 *a controlled, detailed making* |
| **colour** | explores colour & materials freely 👁 *uses colour without prompting* | names & matches colours 👁 *names 3+ colours* | mixes colours & notices the result 👁 *makes a new colour & says what happened* | uses colour with intention — warm/cool, mood 👁 *picks a colour for an effect & says why* |
| **creative expression** | explores ideas & media freely, tries things 👁 *attempts a new idea* | shows a feeling or idea in their work 👁 *says what it's about* | makes deliberate choices — subject, placement 👁 *explains one choice* | brings it together — an intentional personal piece they can talk about 👁 *a piece they planned & describe* |

**★ art north star (3–5):** *makes with control, uses colour with intention, and creates a personal piece they can talk about — looking closely and expressing their own idea, not copying.*

---

## 4 · levels — same activity, every child at their own rung

In **one** making activity, an **L1** child explores the material and makes free marks while an **L3** child plans a composition and chooses colours for a mood — **same activity, different rung.** Level is per-skill attainment; promotion = securing the ★ rung independently, more than once — **not by age.** The reference-artwork making block is naturally open-ended, which is what lets one activity hold all three rungs.

---

## 5 · how it feeds the rest

- **experience book:** the three skills' rungs are exactly what the book tracks per child — the dot-and-date journey toward each ★, with the "needs more help" circle (e.g. a child stuck at "tool precision" for weeks → fine-motor focus). The book is planned from these rungs.
- **activities (already built):** art gym (fine-motor warm-up), art games, reference-artwork making, art care — each runs at all three rungs. The **new segments** to script are only the shared **opening circle / closing reflection** and the **deepened making block moves** (make → look closely → talk about choices, keyed to the creative-expression rungs).

### format-set principle (for the new segments)
Same as language & stem: each new recurring slot gets a **small fixed set of named, scripted formats**, rotated on a known schedule — repetition for the child, no guesswork for the teacher, each format nudging a different sub-skill. (e.g. a fixed set of **"gallery look" prompts** for the look-closely move; a fixed set of **art-care / i-care** closing rituals.)
